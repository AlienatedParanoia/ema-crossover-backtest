Section 4

Results & comparison

All figures below are measured on the out-of-sample test period (Jul 2022 – Dec 2024) with 0.1% per-trade costs applied to the EMA strategy. The benchmark is a passive buy-and-hold (B&H) position in the same stock.

The verdict is stark. Buy-and-hold wins the risk-adjusted comparison (Sharpe) in all five stocks and the absolute-return comparison in all five. The EMA strategy's only win anywhere in the table is Tesco's maximum drawdown (−14.10% vs −26.49%) — a single instance where stepping into cash genuinely cushioned a decline. Everywhere else, the cost of sitting out rallies outweighed any protection the rule provided.
